# Building a One-Person AI Software Team — Short Version

> A condensed, post-length version of [`OVERVIEW.md`](OVERVIEW.md) for sharing.
> The full write-up has the longer treatment; the "tools, and why" appendix lives
> there too.

What if one person could run an entire software team — by putting AI in every seat
except the one that matters most: final judgment?

That's the project. **An AI does the building, a panel of other AIs reviews the
work, and I stay in the chair that decides what ships** — all reachable from any
device, anywhere.

**Where it came from** — a fusion of two philosophies:

- **@levelsio (Pieter Levels)** — the solo operator: run real businesses yourself,
  automate instead of hiring, keep it simple, one always-on server, work from
  anywhere, ship fast. *(The form.)*
- **Naval Ravikant** — leverage: code and automation are a permissionless army that
  works while you sleep, and once leverage is infinite, **judgment becomes the
  scarce human skill**. *(The philosophy.)*

One line: **Naval's leverage, run in levelsio's style.**

**How it works:**

1. **A cloud "headquarters"** that's always on — every device is just a window into
   it. Start a task at my desk, pick it up on my phone; the work never paused.
2. **An AI that builds** — it doesn't hand me a finished thing, it *proposes* its
   work for review.
3. **A council of AI reviewers** — a panel of different models each check from a
   different angle (design, bugs, security, over-complication). They disagree, and
   that's the point: models have different blind spots, so the panel catches what
   one reviewer would miss.

**My role shrinks to the highest-value part: I decide what ships.** Nothing goes
live without a human "go" — "leverage amplifies judgment" made literal: infinite
automated execution funneled through one human point of judgment.

It's safe because everything is **tracked and reversible**, nothing ships
**without review + a human green-light**, and the whole setup is **private to my
own devices**.

The north star the whole way:

> **You stay solo. The agents argue. You ship from your phone.**

**The takeaway worth discussing:** treat AI as a *team*, not a tool — a builder, a
panel of critics, a human judge. Automate the doing and the checking; keep the
judging.
